Why you should love Dr Santos

The Case for Dr Trinity Santos

I'm sure many of us have heard of the new HBO Max hit show The Pitt, set in a Pittsburgh teaching hospital's Emergency Department. The show has only been running for a little over a year now, but already has an incredible fan base. My personal opinion is that it is one of the best medical dramas I've had the pleasure of watching, with deeply complex and well-written characters. But lately, with more and more people watching, I've seen an increase in hate for my personal favourite character, Dr Trinity Santos.


I admit she might be a bit rough around the edges, and yes, she does tease her fellow doctors-in-training a bit harshly, but what I have failed to see is a true reason to hate her character completely. If you haven't seen the show yet, I suggest you do before I spoil it all on this page, and when you come back, I hope you'll either love her as much as I do or at least hear me out while I defend her honour.


We first meet Dr Santos with two other resident doctors, Dr D. Whittaker and Dr V. Javadi, or as Trinity likes to call them, Huckleberry (since D.W. lived his life on a farm) and Crash (since V.J. fainted during their first observation of the shift). Their shift starts with Trinity mocking them as a defence mechanism. Being a doctor is extremely hard, and while she does believe she has the talent and is quite self-arrogant, she still has doubts, just like you and me. She also knows that both of them (especially Dr Javadi) are a threat to her because they all want to get into their chosen residency once they complete school. Dr Javadi already has connections in surgery because of her parents, who are both legends in the department, and Dr Whittaker has seemingly been taken under the wing of their day-shift attending, Dr. "Robby" Robinavitch.

However, Trinity herself has also seemingly been taken under the wing of a talented surgeon, Dr Y. Garcia, although their relationship later becomes unprofessional as they begin seeing each other outside of work. This is all good and dandy except for the other senior resident, Dr F. Langdon, whom Trinity suspected was stealing drugs from the hospital. Trinity's entire first day was spent receiving many, many lectures from Langdon, who ultimately ended up berating her in front of an entire room of people, only to be stopped by Dr Robby. While some of the lectures were justified (as Trinity had a habit of not seeking guidance on patient treatments), Langdon's whole screaming session was completely unjustifiable, especially since we, as the audience, know that it was partly because he was high on pills while working. Trinity ended up reporting Langdon to Dr Robby, which probably saved him from slipping further into his addiction, and considering that Dr Langdon came back to repeat his last year of residency, she saved him from losing his medical license completely.

Trinity also seems to have a soft side for patients who she feels are struggling with things like mental health or abuse. There have been many times when she didn't let go of a case just because she was worried that a little kid was being taken advantage of by an adult. She even shared that the same thing happened to her and a friend when she was younger, and that's the reason why she acts the way she does now. Trinity might think a lot of her patients are stupid people who do even stupider things to land themselves in the hospital, but for the 5% of patients who she knows have something more happening beneath the surface, she steps up and helps in the best way she knows how. Yes, she might be wrong sometimes, like with the girl who had bruises, but it turned out she had immune thrombocytopenic purpura (ITP), a rare autoimmune blood disorder, but that is something I would want in my doctor: a passion to look out for the patient's well-being and safety.


So in short why you should give her a chance:

  1. Trinity is an amazing doctor who looks out for her patients.
  2. Her dislike for Dr Langdon is valid!
  3. How could you hate this gorgeous face??

I hope that more people can see her for the good soul she is and not for the monster people are painting her out to be. I hope even more that she thrives in this show and does not suffer more than she already has. All characters of The Pitt belong to HBO, and this is just my personal opinion.
